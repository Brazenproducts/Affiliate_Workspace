# Emergency Shelter / Disaster Housing — Market Research (4/22/2026)

## The Opportunity
Municipalities (especially hurricane-prone FL, TX, LA, NC) purchase emergency shelters and temporary/transitional housing in bulk. Two angles:
1. **Brand listed as top choice** on review/comparison websites
2. **New minority disabled veteran-owned company** as preferred vendor (huge advantage in government procurement — see SBA 8(a), SDVOSB set-asides)

## Market Segments (Two Distinct Categories)

### A. Storm Shelters / Safe Rooms (Hardened Protection)
These are reinforced structures designed to protect people DURING a storm. FEMA P-361 / ICC 500-2020 compliant.

**Key Players:**
- **STORMBOX** (stormbox.com) — Above-ground, prefabricated steel community safe rooms. 27-person and 49-person models. FEMA 361 & ICC 500 compliant. Tested at Texas Tech Wind Institute. Targets municipalities, schools, corporate campuses. Relocatable. (833) 937-8485.
- **US Tornado Shelter** — Residential and community safe rooms
- **Survive-a-Storm** — Residential and community shelters, FEMA compliant
- **Titan Florida Doors** — Storm shelter doors specifically for FL market

### B. Disaster Relief Housing / Temporary Shelters (Post-Storm Housing)
These are livable structures deployed AFTER a disaster for displaced families. HUD-code manufactured homes, modular units, rapid-deploy shelters.

**Top 5 Providers (per industry analysis):**
1. **Clayton Homes** (Berkshire Hathaway) — FEMA's #1 supplier since Katrina. ~$15B valuation. Tens of thousands of units delivered. The 800-lb gorilla.
2. **Cavco Industries** (publicly traded, ~$4.3B) — Major FEMA partner. Delivered thousands of HUD-code homes post-Harvey, Laura, Ida. Absorbed Fleetwood Homes.
3. **Skyline Champion** (~$4.0B) — Nationwide factory network, consistent FEMA/state supplier.
4. **NanoNest Homes** (nanonests.com, Hawaii startup, ~$125M est.) — ICC-compliant steel-frame modular. 160 homes in Lahaina, 450-home Ka La'i Ola project. The disruptor.
5. **Boxabl** (boxabl.com, Las Vegas) — Foldable modular homes, ships on a truck. Consumer + government. High media profile.

**Other Notable Players:**
- **Pallet Shelter** (palletshelter.com) — Public Benefit Corp. Rapid-deploy transitional shelter villages. Focused on homelessness but applicable to disaster. "Built in a day."
- **Prefabex** — Modular/container-based emergency housing
- **CHS (Container Housing Solutions)** — Shipping container conversions

## FEMA Funding Channels (How Municipalities Pay)
- **HMGP (Hazard Mitigation Grant Program)** — Post-disaster. FEMA funds up to 75% of eligible costs. Applied through State Hazard Mitigation Officer (SHMO).
- **PDM (Pre-Disaster Mitigation)** — Nationally competitive. FEMA funds 75-90%, up to $3M federal share per project.
- **CDBG (Community Development Block Grant)** — HUD-funded. For tornado shelters in manufactured home parks (20+ units, low/moderate income).
- **FHA 203(k)** — Rehabilitation loans that can include windstorm shelters.
- **BRIC (Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities)** — Annual competitive grant.

## Compliance Requirements
- **FEMA P-361 (2021)** — Design and construction guidance for safe rooms
- **ICC 500-2020** — Standard for storm shelters (referenced by FEMA P-361)
- As of Jan 1, 2024: ALL FEMA-funded prefabricated safe rooms MUST comply with ICC 500-2020
- **FEMA does NOT endorse, approve, certify, or recommend any specific contractors or products.** Cannot legally say "FEMA approved" — say "FEMA P-361 compliant" or "meets ICC 500-2020 standards"

## Veteran-Owned Business Advantage (MASSIVE for Government Procurement)
- **SDVOSB (Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business)** — Federal set-aside contracts
- **SBA 8(a) Program** — If minority + disadvantaged, can get sole-source contracts up to $4.5M
- **VA Vets First** — VA contracts prioritize SDVOSBs
- **State-level preferences** — Florida, Texas, and most hurricane states have veteran business preference programs
- **GSA Schedule** — Once on GSA, municipalities can buy directly without full RFP process
- Combined minority + disabled veteran status = strongest possible position for government contracts

## Florida-Specific Context
- Hurricane season: June 1 – November 30
- FL Division of Emergency Management coordinates with FEMA
- FL has its own Hazard Mitigation Grant Program allocation
- Key target counties: Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Lee, Collier, Pinellas, Hillsborough, Volusia, Brevard, Duval
